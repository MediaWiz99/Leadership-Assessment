Three Imperatives Leadership Assessment

Discover your strengths and areas for growth as a leader

Manage Yourself

1. Do you use your formal authority effectively?

This is a strength if: You consider it a useful tool but not your primary means of influencing others. You make clear why you do what you do and share your authority when appropriate.
Area for development: Focus on using your formal authority as a leadership tool rather than relying on it as your primary influence method. Work on sharing authority appropriately.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Effective leaders use authority as a last resort, not a first option. Try leading through influence, vision, and inspiration before relying on positional power. When you do use authority, explain the "why" behind your decisions to maintain trust and buy-in.

2. Do you create thoughtful but not overly personal relationships?

This is a strength if: Your relationships are rich in human connections but always focused on the purpose and goals of the team and organization. You avoid trying to influence people by befriending them.
Area for development: Work on finding the right balance between building genuine human connections and maintaining appropriate professional boundaries.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Professional warmth is key. Show genuine interest in your team members' growth and well-being while keeping conversations aligned with work objectives. Remember: you can care without becoming friends, and you can be friendly without being personal.

3. Do others trust you as a manager?

This is a strength if: People, particularly your own, believe in your competence, intentions, and values. You demonstrate concern for their individual success.
Area for development: Focus on building trust through demonstrating both competence and character. Show genuine concern for your people's individual success.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Trust is earned through consistent actions over time. Be reliable in your commitments, transparent in your communication, and demonstrate that you have your team's best interests at heart. When you make mistakes, own them quickly and learn publicly.

4. Do you exercise your influence ethically?

This is a strength if: You consistently identify stakeholders, weigh their interests, and try to mitigate any harm that your actions may cause as you attempt to accomplish a greater good.
Area for development: Practice systematically considering all stakeholders affected by your decisions. Work on weighing different interests and minimizing harm.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Before making important decisions, create a stakeholder map. Ask yourself: "Who will be affected by this decision?" and "How can I minimize negative impact while achieving the greater good?" Ethical leadership means considering long-term consequences, not just immediate results.

Manage Your Network

5. Do you systematically identify those who should be in your network?

This is a strength if: You are always aware of which people and groups you and your team depend on, and vice versa, as circumstances change.
Area for development: Create a systematic approach to mapping your stakeholder network. Regularly assess who you depend on and who depends on you.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Create a network map quarterly. Include peers, superiors, customers, suppliers, and influencers. Ask: "Who can help me succeed?" and "Who needs my support to succeed?" Update this map as your role and responsibilities evolve.

6. Do you proactively build and maintain your network?

This is a strength if: You create and sustain relationships with those in your network, connect frequently with them, and support their needs.
Area for development: Be more proactive about building and maintaining professional relationships. Schedule regular check-ins with key network members.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Schedule monthly "relationship maintenance" time. Send updates, share relevant articles, or simply check in. Remember: networking is about giving value, not just receiving it. Look for ways to help others succeed, and they'll be more willing to help you.

7. Do you use your network to provide the protection and resources your team needs?

This is a strength if: You effectively leverage your network to shield your team from unnecessary bureaucracy and secure the resources they need to excel.
Area for development: Work on becoming a more effective advocate for your team. Use your relationships to remove obstacles and secure resources.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Your network should serve your team's success, not just your own. Regularly ask your team: "What obstacles can I remove?" and "What resources do you need?" Then use your relationships to deliver solutions. This builds tremendous loyalty and trust.

Manage Your Team

8. Do you foster a collaborative team environment?

This is a strength if: You create an environment where team members work together effectively, share knowledge freely, and support each other's success.
Area for development: Focus on breaking down silos and encouraging cross-functional collaboration. Create more opportunities for team members to work together.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Model collaborative behavior by asking for input, sharing credit generously, and making team decisions transparently. Create regular opportunities for cross-training and joint problem-solving. Celebrate team wins, not just individual achievements.

9. Do you effectively communicate vision and direction?

This is a strength if: Your team understands the bigger picture, knows how their work contributes to organizational goals, and feels motivated by the shared vision.
Area for development: Work on articulating a clear, compelling vision and connecting daily tasks to larger organizational objectives.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Use storytelling to make your vision memorable and meaningful. Regularly connect individual tasks to the bigger picture by saying "Here's how your work on X helps us achieve Y." Repeat key messages frequently - people need to hear important information multiple times.

10. Do you develop and empower your team members?

This is a strength if: You actively invest in your team's growth, delegate meaningfully, and give people the autonomy to make decisions within their areas of responsibility.
Area for development: Focus more on coaching and developing your team members. Look for opportunities to delegate challenging assignments that promote growth.
💡 Leadership Tip:

Ask each team member: "What's one skill you'd like to develop this year?" Then create opportunities for them to practice it. Delegate not just tasks, but decision-making authority. When people make mistakes, use them as coaching moments rather than reasons to take back control.
